# Securities, mutual funds, and trading

## Collect the right reports

- Use a capital-gains statement with purchase lots, sale dates, quantities, proceeds, cost, holding period, and STT status.
- A redemption confirmation shows proceeds, not taxable gain.
- For non-demat mutual funds, use MF Central, CAMS, KFintech, the AMC, or a consolidated account statement. CDSL or NSDL alone may not contain every folio.
- Reconcile broker tax P&L, ledger charges, and contract notes.

## Listed equity and equity-oriented mutual funds

- Report STT-paid short-term and long-term gains in the applicable Schedule CG sections.
- Populate Schedule 112A when required, even if the net long-term gain falls below the exemption threshold.
- Use transaction-level or permitted aggregate reporting exactly as the current utility requires.
- Ensure Schedule CG totals flow into computation schedules and special-rate income.

## Losses

- Classify delivery capital losses separately from speculative intraday loss and non-speculative F&O loss.
- Apply current-year set-off rules before brought-forward losses.
- Claim a brought-forward loss only if it arose in a validly filed earlier return and remains eligible. A broker report alone does not create a tax loss carry-forward.
- Do not enter old losses from memory; use prior acknowledgements and filed-return schedules.

## Intraday and F&O turnover

Trading turnover is not the gross contract value and is not necessarily the same as profit or loss. Prefer the broker's tax P&L turnover computed under the applicable professional guidance.

- Intraday equity activity is generally speculative business.
- Eligible exchange-traded derivatives are generally non-speculative business.
- For derivatives, turnover commonly includes absolute favorable and unfavorable differences and other components required by current guidance; option premium treatment depends on the applicable method and reporting period.
- Do not force a portal validation by converting a loss into positive income or setting turnover equal to profit.
- If the broker report and portal fields cannot be reconciled economically, stop and obtain a computation from a tax professional.

Portal P&L may require gross-profit and expense fields to express a loss. Preserve the economic result and verify that Schedule BP classifies it correctly.
