# Income tax versus FEMA and RBI compliance

Income-tax reporting and FEMA/RBI/AD-bank compliance are separate lanes.

## Income-tax lane

Determine from the current AY instructions and residential status whether the return requires:

- Schedule FA for foreign assets/accounts/interests;
- Schedule FSI and Schedule TR for foreign income and foreign-tax relief;
- unlisted-equity disclosure;
- director/partner disclosures;
- the foreign-asset question in Part B-TTI;
- income under salary, capital gains, other sources, or business/profession.

Schedule FA generally concerns resident ordinarily resident taxpayers and uses the calendar year ending 31 December. Verify exact current instructions. Use the form's legal-owner, beneficial-owner, beneficiary, or other defined terminology; do not improvise ownership labels.

## FEMA/RBI/AD-bank lane

Separately determine whether the facts need review under overseas-investment, LRS, ODI/OPI, reporting, valuation, UIN, APR, or AD-bank requirements.

Ask:

- How was the asset acquired: purchase, subscription, ESOP, gift, inheritance, merger, or another route?
- Is the taxpayer a legal owner, beneficial owner, beneficiary, director, controller, or holder of a material ownership percentage?
- Was any remittance made under LRS?
- Does an AD bank, UIN, Form FC, ODI/OPI filing, valuation, APR, or pending acknowledgement exist?
- Were shares transferred, sold, or gifted?

Keep share certificates, gift deeds, valuation records, entity registers, remittance records, AD-bank correspondence, UIN/ODI/OPI/LRS records, and filed acknowledgements.

## Separation rule

- Completing Schedule FA does not cure, certify, or regularize FEMA compliance.
- An ODI/LRS/AD-bank record does not complete the ITR disclosure or tax computation.
- A foreign-company share is a security, not a VDA.
- A gift may have one income-tax treatment while still requiring asset disclosure and regulatory review.

Foreign gifts, unlisted foreign shares, control/director interests, uncertain valuation, missing or pending AD-bank filings, or unclear ODI/OPI treatment require specialist review before submission.
